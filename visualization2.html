Age Distribution by Diagnosis (Dropdown Country Filter)

This interactive violin plot displays how age is distributed among individuals with or without Alzheimer’s, based on the selected country (USA, Canada, or Mexico). The country dropdown lets you view the distribution of ages across diagnosed vs. non-diagnosed individuals in different countries. These distributions help visualize how Alzheimer’s manifests across age ranges in different populations.

Interpretation

In all three populations (USA, Canada, and Mexico), individuals without Alzheimer’s tend to be younger, while those with a diagnosis cluster in the older age ranges, particularly between ages 75 and 90. This reinforces the well-documented association between aging and increased risk of Alzheimer’s. The interactive country filter allows viewers to observe subtle differences between regions — for instance, Mexico’s diagnosed population appears slightly older on average, while the USA and Canada show more gradual shifts. These insights emphasize the critical need for early intervention strategies, especially as national populations continue to age at varying rates.
