Global Prevalence of Alzheimer’s Diagnosis

This interactive choropleth map illustrates the percentage of individuals diagnosed with Alzheimer’s disease across countries worldwide. Darker shades indicate a higher percentage of the population diagnosed. By visualizing geographic variation in diagnosis prevalence, we can better understand disparities that may stem from healthcare access, environmental factors, or genetic predisposition.

Interpretation

Countries like Mexico and the United States show higher prevalence, likely due to a combination of age demographics, awareness, diagnosis rates, and underlying health and genetic risk factors. Lighter-colored countries either have lower prevalence or are not represented in the dataset.
