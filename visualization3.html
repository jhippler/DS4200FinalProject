Categorical Alzheimer’s Factor Analysis in North America

This interactive bar plot displays the proportion of people with or without Alzheimer’s, who have different categorical risk factors.

Interpretation

Each category — from sleep quality and dietary habits to smoking status and stress levels — shows meaningful differences in the percentage of individuals diagnosed with Alzheimer’s. For instance, individuals with poor sleep quality, high stress, or low social engagement appear to have a higher proportion of Alzheimer’s diagnoses compared to their counterparts. Similarly, factors like current smoking or unhealthy dietary habits show stronger associations with diagnosis rates. While this visualization does not establish causation, it provides a compelling overview of behavioral and environmental patterns that may influence risk. It emphasizes the importance of holistic public health strategies targeting mental wellness, lifestyle improvements, and environmental exposures in Alzheimer’s prevention efforts.
