Risk Factors Across North American Countries

This scatter plot displays prevalence of various risk factors across North American Countries (USA, Canada, or Mexico). This scatterplot visualizes the percentage of individuals within each country (USA, Mexico, and Canada) who present key risk factors for Alzheimer’s, including diabetes, hypertension, family history, and the presence of the APOE-ε4 genetic allele. Each dot represents the prevalence of a specific risk factor in a given country, enabling easy cross-country comparisons of health-related vulnerabilities.

Interpretation

This chart reveals consistent patterns in risk factor prevalence across North America. Notably, family history of Alzheimer’s and hypertension are the most common among the population in all three countries, each affecting around 30% or more of individuals. Meanwhile, genetic risk and diabetes hover closer to 20%. While the differences across countries are relatively minor, Mexico shows slightly higher levels of hypertension and genetic risk compared to the USA and Canada. These insights underscore the importance of both hereditary and metabolic health in assessing Alzheimer’s risk and highlight the need for public health strategies that address common comorbidities across national populations.
